About Badian NHS

Welcome to Badian National High School!

We are a public secondary school located in the beautiful town of Badian, Cebu, Philippines. Our school was established in 1964 and has since been committed to providing quality education to our students.

At Badian National High School, we believe that education is not just about academics, but also about character formation and holistic development. Our faculty and staff are dedicated to nurturing our students into well-rounded individuals who are equipped with the knowledge, skills, and values necessary to succeed in life.

We offer a wide range of academic programs that cater to the different interests and career paths of our students. Our curriculum is designed to meet the standards set by the Department of Education, ensuring that our students receive a high-quality education that is recognized both nationally and internationally.

Apart from academics, we also offer various extracurricular activities that allow our students to explore their interests and develop their talents. We have clubs and organizations for sports, music, arts, and more, as well as community service programs that encourage our students to give back to their community.

At Badian National High School, we value diversity and inclusivity. We welcome students from different backgrounds and cultures, and we strive to create a safe and supportive learning environment where everyone feels valued and respected.

Thank you for considering Badian National High School for your education. We look forward to welcoming you into our school community!

History

A Brief History of the School

Badian National High School, located in the municipality of Badian, Cebu, has a rich history that spans several decades. It was established on July 1, 1948, under the leadership of then-Mayor Vicente Y. Reyes. Initially, the school began as a small institution known as Badian Rural High School, offering secondary education to the local community.

Over the years, Badian Rural High School underwent several transformations and expansions to accommodate the growing number of students. In 1960, the school was renamed Badian Municipal High School, reflecting its status as a key educational institution in the region. As the demand for quality education continued to rise, the school underwent further development, with additional classrooms, facilities, and academic programs being introduced.

In 1995, Badian Municipal High School was elevated to the status of a national high school, leading to its current name, Badian National High School. This change in status allowed the school to offer a broader range of academic programs and attract students from neighboring towns and municipalities.

Throughout its history, Badian National High School has been committed to providing excellent education to its students. It has produced numerous graduates who have excelled in various fields, including academics, sports, and the arts. The school's dedicated faculty and staff have played a crucial role in shaping the minds and talents of the students, ensuring their holistic development.

Today, Badian National High School continues to be a pillar of education in the municipality, providing a conducive learning environment for its diverse student population. The school prides itself on its strong academic curriculum, extracurricular activities, and commitment to producing well-rounded individuals who are equipped with the necessary skills to succeed in their chosen paths.

As Badian National High School looks towards the future, it remains dedicated to its mission of molding future leaders and contributing to the growth and progress of the community.

Our Mission & Vision

The DepEd Mission

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where:
Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment.
Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner.

Our Mandate

The Department of Education was established through the Education Decree of 1863 as the Superior Commission of Primary Instruction under a Chairman. The Education agency underwent many reorganization efforts in the 20th century in order to better define its purpose vis a vis the changing administrations and charters. The present day Department of Education was eventually mandated through Republic Act 9155, otherwise known as the Governance of Basic Education act of 2001 which establishes the mandate of this agency.

The Department of Education (DepEd) formulates, implements, and coordinates policies, plans, programs and projects in the areas of formal and non-formal basic education. It supervises all elementary and secondary education institutions, including alternative learning systems, both public and private; and provides for the establishment and maintenance of a complete, adequate, and integrated system of basic education relevant to the goals of national development.
